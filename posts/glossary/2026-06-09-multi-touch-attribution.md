Multi-touch attribution is an attribution approach that distributes conversion credit across multiple channels, campaigns, pages, or touchpoints in a customer journey. It recognizes that many conversions are influenced by more than one interaction.

For example, a customer might discover a brand through organic search, compare alternatives through a review site, return from email, and finally convert from direct traffic.

## Why multi-touch attribution matters

Multi-touch attribution helps teams understand the full journey rather than only the final click. It can reveal channels that assist conversions, educate visitors, or build trust before the final action.

This is useful for B2B, SaaS, high-consideration ecommerce, and any business with longer journeys.

## Common multi-touch models

Common models include:

- Linear attribution
- Time-decay attribution
- Position-based attribution
- First and last touch weighting
- Data-driven attribution

Each model answers a different question. There is no universally perfect model.

## Limitations

Multi-touch attribution still has blind spots. Privacy restrictions, multiple devices, dark social, offline sales, and missing UTM tags can make journeys incomplete.
