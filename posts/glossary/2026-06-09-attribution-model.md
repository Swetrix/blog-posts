An attribution model is a set of rules for deciding which traffic source, campaign, or touchpoint gets credit for a conversion. If someone discovers your product through organic search, returns from a newsletter, clicks a retargeting ad, and then signs up directly, attribution decides how credit is assigned.

Attribution models help marketers and founders understand which channels influence conversions. They also shape budget decisions, campaign reporting, and how teams judge the value of content, ads, partnerships, email, search, and social media.

## Common attribution models

The most common attribution models include:

| Model             | How credit is assigned                                                                  |
| ----------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| First interaction | The first known touchpoint gets full credit.                                            |
| Last interaction  | The final known touchpoint before conversion gets full credit.                          |
| Linear            | Credit is split evenly across known touchpoints.                                        |
| Time decay        | Later touchpoints receive more credit than earlier ones.                                |
| Position based    | The first and last touchpoints receive the most credit, with the rest split in between. |
| Data driven       | A platform estimates credit based on observed conversion patterns.                      |

Each model answers a different question. First interaction helps you understand discovery. Last interaction helps you understand what closes. Linear and position-based models are better for longer journeys where several touches matter.

## Why attribution is difficult

Attribution is never perfect. Browsers restrict referrer data, visitors use multiple devices, links get shared privately, privacy rules limit tracking, and many conversions happen after offline conversations. Direct traffic can also hide visits that actually came from apps, documents, messaging tools, or untagged campaigns.

This is why attribution should be treated as a decision aid, not an exact truth machine. The goal is to compare patterns, spot useful channels, and improve campaign tagging.

## How to improve attribution quality

Use consistent UTM codes on campaigns you control. Keep source and medium values clean. Track conversions as goals or custom events. Review traffic channels alongside landing pages, referrers, and conversion rate.
