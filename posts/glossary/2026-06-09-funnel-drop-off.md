Funnel drop-off is the point where visitors leave a sequence before completing the next step or final conversion. It is one of the most useful signals in conversion analytics because it shows where a journey breaks down.

For example, if 1,000 visitors start checkout, 600 enter payment details, and 300 complete payment, the funnel has major drop-off between payment entry and completion.

## Common funnel drop-off points

Drop-offs often happen at:

- Signup forms
- Email verification
- Pricing pages
- Checkout pages
- Payment steps
- Onboarding tasks
- Feature setup screens
- Download gates
- Contact forms

Each drop-off point suggests a different kind of friction.

## Why funnel drop-off happens

Drop-off can be caused by unclear copy, weak intent, slow pages, broken forms, payment failures, unexpected costs, required account creation, poor mobile UX, or JavaScript errors.

That is why funnel analysis should be connected with performance and error tracking, not just marketing reports.

## How to reduce funnel drop-off

Start with the largest drop-off that affects business value. Segment by device, source, country, browser, and campaign. Review errors and page speed. Then test focused improvements.
