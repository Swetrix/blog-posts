Goal tracking is the setup and measurement of important outcomes on a website or product. Goals turn raw traffic data into business context by showing whether visitors complete the actions that matter.

A goal can be a page view, custom event, form submission, purchase, signup, download, upgrade, or product activation step.

## Goal tracking examples

Common goals include:

- Trial signup
- Purchase completed
- Demo booked
- Contact form submitted
- Newsletter subscription
- File downloaded
- Account created
- Project created
- Plan upgraded
- Checkout completed

Goals can be macro conversions or micro conversions. Macro goals represent primary outcomes, while micro goals show progress.

## Why goal tracking matters

Without goals, analytics can show where visitors came from and which pages they viewed, but not whether the website succeeded. Goal tracking lets you compare traffic sources, campaigns, pages, devices, and countries by outcome quality.

For example, one channel may bring many visitors but few signups. Another may bring fewer visitors but a higher conversion rate.

## How to set up goals

Choose goals that map to real business decisions. Track completed actions, not just clicks, where possible. Connect goals to funnels and segments so you can see where visitors drop off.
