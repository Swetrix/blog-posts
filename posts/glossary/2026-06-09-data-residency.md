Data residency describes the physical or legal region where data is stored and processed. For website analytics, this can include event data, visitor data, account data, logs, backups, and reporting databases.

Data residency matters because privacy laws, customer contracts, industry policies, and internal security rules may restrict where data can be stored or transferred.

## Why data residency matters

Data residency can affect:

- GDPR compliance posture
- Vendor reviews
- Enterprise procurement
- Government or healthcare requirements
- Data transfer assessments
- Customer trust
- Incident response
- Support and subprocessors

For many EU-focused businesses, keeping analytics data in the EU is an important requirement.

## Data residency vs data sovereignty

Data residency is about where data is stored. Data sovereignty is about which laws and authorities may govern that data. They are related, but not identical.

A company may care about both the region where analytics data sits and the legal framework that applies to the provider.

## Analytics data residency

Analytics platforms can collect sensitive behavioral and technical data. Teams should understand where that data goes, which subprocessors are used, and whether data leaves required regions.
