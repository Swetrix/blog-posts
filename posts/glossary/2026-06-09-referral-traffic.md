Referral traffic is traffic that arrives after a visitor clicks a link from another website. If a blog, partner directory, review site, documentation page, forum, or news article links to your site and someone clicks it, that visit can appear as referral traffic.

Referral traffic helps you understand which external websites are sending visitors and whether those visitors engage, convert, or bring revenue.

## How referral traffic is tracked

When a visitor clicks a link, the browser may send referrer information to the destination website. Analytics tools can use this referrer to identify the source domain or page.

For example, if a visitor clicks from example-blog.com to your pricing page, your analytics platform may show example-blog.com as a referrer.

Referrer data can be limited by browser privacy rules, referrer policies, HTTPS settings, apps, redirects, and adblockers. Because of this, referral reports are useful but not perfect.

## Referral traffic examples

Referral traffic can come from:

- Review sites
- Partner pages
- Blog posts
- Forums
- Documentation links
- Affiliate sites
- Directories
- News articles
- Community websites
- Customer websites

Some analytics platforms separate social and search traffic from referrals, even though they also involve links from another domain.

## How to analyze referral traffic

Look beyond visit count. Good referral analysis includes bounce rate, conversion rate, landing pages, events, revenue, and returning visitors. A small referral source with a high conversion rate may be more valuable than a large source with low-quality traffic.
