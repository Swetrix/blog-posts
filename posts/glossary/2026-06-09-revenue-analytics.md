Revenue analytics is the analysis of revenue alongside traffic, campaigns, conversions, users, and product behavior. It helps teams understand not only what drives visits or signups, but what drives money.

Revenue analytics is especially useful for SaaS, ecommerce, subscription products, marketplaces, and paid content businesses.

## What revenue analytics tracks

Revenue analytics can include:

- Purchases
- Subscriptions
- Upgrades
- Monthly recurring revenue
- Annual recurring revenue
- Revenue by source
- Revenue by campaign
- Revenue by landing page
- Revenue by user segment
- Refunds or churn context

The exact metrics depend on the business model.

## Why revenue analytics matters

Traffic and signups can be misleading if they do not produce revenue. One campaign may generate many trial users but few paying customers. Another campaign may bring fewer visitors but higher-value accounts.

Revenue analytics helps teams invest in channels and pages that produce profitable outcomes.

## Revenue analytics and privacy

Revenue data can be sensitive. Teams should avoid exposing unnecessary personal data and should connect payment systems carefully.
