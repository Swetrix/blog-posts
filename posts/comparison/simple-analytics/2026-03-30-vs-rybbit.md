As website owners increasingly seek out privacy-friendly analytics, two interesting options have emerged: [Simple Analytics](https://simpleanalytics.com), a veteran of the extreme-minimalism movement, and [Rybbit](https://rybbit.com), a newer, open-source contender trying to provide deeper technical features without sacrificing privacy.

Both platforms share a noble goal: eliminate cookies, banish intrusive tracking, and respect user privacy while giving you the core traffic metrics you need. However, they take vastly different approaches when it comes to feature depth and software openness.

In this breakdown, we will compare Simple Analytics and Rybbit side-by-side. Then, we will introduce [Swetrix](https://swetrix.com), a mature, fully-featured alternative that offers the professional growth tools both of these platforms lack.

## Simple Analytics Overview

Simple Analytics has built its reputation on extreme minimalism. It avoids tracking anything that could identify a user, giving you an aggregated, clutter-free dashboard. It is designed for users who want to glance at their pageviews and move on with their day, without ever worrying about GDPR compliance or cookie banners.

![Simple Analytics dashboard screenshot](https://cdn.swetrix.com/file/6b3c2198630ee67799fce8b023fa4137.png)

**Strengths:**

- **Highly Intuitive:** Incredibly simple dashboard that anyone can instantly understand.
- **AI Chat Query:** Features a natural language AI assistant to ask specific questions about your data.
- **Privacy Centric:** Guarantees compliance by refusing to store any personal data.

**Cons:**

- **Closed Ecosystem:** It is a proprietary, closed-source product, meaning you cannot host it yourself.
- **Lacks Deep User Behavior:** Offers almost no tools for tracking full user sessions, capturing city-level geolocation data, or visualizing deep user flow diagrams.

## Rybbit Overview

Rybbit is a newcomer to the open-source analytics space. Unlike Simple Analytics, it provides more technical depth by including features like error tracking and performance monitoring. It aims to bridge the gap between basic traffic counters and complex developer tools, wrapped in a modern UI.

![Rybbit dashboard screenshot](https://cdn.swetrix.com/file/a12f63b09d54aa4521477007ce4c5031.png)

**Strengths:**

- **Open Source Freedom:** Fully open-source and self-hostable, giving you total control over your infrastructure.
- **Technical Insights:** Tracks page load speeds and JavaScript errors directly in the dashboard.
- **Visual Depth:** Includes user flow analysis to help you understand how visitors navigate your site.

**Cons:**

- **Missing Security Essentials:** Lacks Two-Factor Authentication (2FA), which is a massive liability for business data.
- **No Proactive Alerts:** If your website traffic spikes or a script breaks, you will not be notified; you must log in to find out.

## Where Both Platforms Leave Gaps

Simple Analytics and Rybbit both offer excellent privacy-first tracking, but they fall short if you want to actively optimize and secure your business.

### 1. Zero Actionable Growth Tools

Both platforms are excellent at showing you historical data, but neither gives you the tools to change those numbers. You cannot run A/B Tests, implement Feature Flags, or track revenue natively in either dashboard. You are left staring at data without a way to experiment with it.

### 2. Lack of Real-Time Monitoring and Alerts

If you launch a new feature that breaks your checkout button, Rybbit will log the error, but it won't tell you about it. Simple Analytics won't track the error at all. Neither platform offers real-time proactive alerts (via email, Slack, or Discord), meaning you have to constantly check the dashboard manually to ensure your site is functioning properly.

### 3. Missing Security Standards

Analytics data is incredibly sensitive. Astonishingly, both Simple Analytics and Rybbit lack Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) support, relying entirely on a simple password to protect your business intelligence.
