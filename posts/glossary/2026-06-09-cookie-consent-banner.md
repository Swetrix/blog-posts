A cookie consent banner is a notice shown on a website that asks visitors to accept, reject, or configure cookies and similar tracking technologies. It is often part of a consent management platform and is used to help websites meet privacy and electronic communications rules.

Cookie banners are common because many analytics, advertising, personalization, and retargeting tools store cookies or access browser storage.

## What a cookie banner does

A cookie banner usually explains:

- Which categories of cookies are used
- Why the cookies are used
- Which vendors receive data
- How visitors can accept or reject tracking
- How visitors can change preferences later

Some banners show a simple accept or reject choice. Others include detailed preference centers with categories such as necessary, analytics, marketing, and personalization.

## Why cookie banners affect analytics

If analytics depends on consent, data may only be collected after the visitor accepts. This can reduce reported page views, sessions, events, and conversions. It can also create bias because privacy-conscious visitors may reject tracking at higher rates than other visitors.

This means analytics after consent is not always a full picture of website traffic. It is a picture of the visitors who allowed measurement.

## How to reduce banner friction

The best way to reduce consent friction is to collect less data. If your analytics setup does not use cookies, does not perform cross-site tracking, and does not collect unnecessary personal data, your consent requirements may be simpler depending on jurisdiction and setup.
