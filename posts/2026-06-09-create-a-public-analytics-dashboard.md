## Privacy And GDPR Compliance

Broadcasting traditional analytics violates GDPR and betrays user trust. Standard platforms collect raw IP addresses, granular geolocation data, and cross-site tracking cookies. Publishing this personal identifiable information (PII) constitutes a severe data breach.

You solve this blind spot using Swetrix.

Server configurations prevent any individual session from tracing back to a specific person. 

Your dashboard achieves GDPR compliance by design because the platform processes anonymous aggregates. Remove the cookie consent banner from your site. Visitors experience a fast website without dismissing legal pop-ups, while you gather accurate metrics without breaking privacy laws.

Brands prioritize privacy-led marketing to build loyalty. Market analysts project the [global privacy-enhancing technologies sector to reach $12.09 billion by 2030](https://www.globenewswire.com/news-release/2023/04/18/2648792/0/en/Privacy-Enhancing-Technologies-Market-Size-Worth-12-09-Billion-By-2030-Grand-View-Research-Inc.html). ## How To Set Up Your Public Dashboard

Creating a public dashboard requires deliberate configuration. Exposing every data point gives competitors an advantage. Granular visibility controls protect your strategy while delivering transparency to your users.

### Choosing The Right Metrics To Share

Select specific widgets for public display to dictate the narrative. Swetrix includes toggles to activate or deactivate individual metrics. 

Share top-line aggregates to demonstrate momentum. Hide granular acquisition data to protect your marketing playbook. Competitors will clone your strategy if they see your exact cost-per-click or top-performing UTM campaigns.

| Metric Category | Public Status | Rationale |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Total Pageviews | Public | Proves overall scale and platform adoption. |
| Unique Visitors | Public | Demonstrates true reach without inflating return visits. |
| Referrers (Domains) | Public | Shows community support and organic mentions. |
| UTM Parameters | Private | Protects paid ad targeting and email campaign specifics. |
| Conversion Goals | Private | Secures sensitive revenue or lead generation rates. |
| Custom Events | Selective | Share product usage milestones; hide billing events. |

Open your Swetrix project settings. Locate the Public Dashboard section. Toggle the switch to enable public access, then select the specific checkboxes for the widgets you wish to expose. Generate [custom UTM parameters](https://swetrix.com/tools/utm-generator) for internal tracking while keeping the UTM report widget hidden from public view.

### Filtering Internal Traffic For Accuracy

Internal teams skew the data. Developers refreshing pages and marketers testing forms inflate your traffic numbers. Public dashboards require strict data integrity. Investors lose confidence if they check your public link and see an unexplained spike caused by your quality assurance team.

Filter internal traffic before exposing your charts.

1.  Identify the static IP addresses for your corporate office.
2.  Gather the static IP addresses of remote contractors and team members.
3.  Open your Swetrix dashboard and click on the project settings.
4.  Enter the IP addresses into the exclusion list.
5.  Save the configuration to block future internal hits.

Implement CIDR block exclusions for larger teams operating on a shared network subnet. This setup prevents internal activity from reaching your public charts.
