When searching for a privacy friendly, cookie-less alternative to Google Analytics, [Pirsch](https://pirsch.io) and [Simple Analytics](https://simpleanalytics.com) are two of the most popular choices. Both tools are built in Europe, prioritize GDPR compliance, and aim to make understanding your website traffic as easy as possible.

While they share a similar philosophy—stripping away complexity in favor of clean, fast dashboards—they differ significantly in their approach to software openness and advanced functionality.

Let us compare Pirsch and Simple Analytics to see which is right for you, and explore why [Swetrix](https://swetrix.com) might be the ultimate choice for those who need a bit more power under the hood.

## Pirsch Overview

Pirsch is a fast, lightweight analytics tool that places a strong emphasis on developer experience. It offers a very clean dashboard and provides excellent API documentation, making it easy to integrate traffic data directly into your own applications or custom dashboards.

![Pirsch dashboard screenshot](https://cdn.swetrix.com/file/6332fa0970da1f2826f08f9c12fe8aa1.png)

**Strengths:**

- **Developer Friendly:** Excellent API and strong integration capabilities.
- **Proactive Alerts:** Easily configure alerts for traffic spikes or specific events.
- **Basic Funnels:** Supports simple multi step conversion tracking.

**Cons:**

- **Open Core Only:** While parts of the software are open source, fully self hosting it requires enterprise setups or significant technical hurdles.
- **No Technical Metrics:** Lacks any insight into page performance or website errors.

## Simple Analytics Overview

As the name suggests, Simple Analytics takes minimalism to the extreme. It strips out everything but the absolute most essential traffic data, ensuring total compliance with privacy laws. It focuses strictly on aggregated pageviews, referrers, and device types without tracking specific user sessions.

![Simple Analytics dashboard screenshot](https://cdn.swetrix.com/file/6b3c2198630ee67799fce8b023fa4137.png)

**Strengths:**

- **Extreme Simplicity:** There is zero learning curve; the dashboard is incredibly easy to read.
- **AI Assistant:** Includes an AI chat feature allowing you to query your data using natural language.
- **Strong Privacy Stance:** Their commitment to minimal data collection is unparalleled.

**Cons:**

- **Closed Source:** Completely proprietary software; you cannot audit the code or self host it.
- **Too Basic:** The lack of funnels, user flow analysis, and deep segmentation can be frustrating as your website grows.

## Where Both Platforms Fall Short

Pirsch and Simple Analytics excel at basic visitor counting, but they hit a wall when you need to dig deeper into website optimization.

### 1. Blind Spots on Performance

Neither Pirsch nor Simple Analytics tracks Core Web Vitals, page load speeds, or JavaScript errors. If your site is painfully slow for users on mobile devices or broken entirely by a faulty script, these tools will not notify you.

### 2. Missing Growth and Testing Tools

Analytics should help you improve your site, not just observe it. Both tools completely lack built in A/B testing and feature flag management, forcing you to rely on external, often expensive, third party solutions.

### 3. Open Source Compromises

If open source transparency is important to you, both tools have drawbacks. Simple Analytics is completely closed source. Pirsch is open core, meaning full self hosting is restricted and complex compared to truly open source software.
