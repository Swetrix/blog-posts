## Privacy Risks of Public Dashboards

Publishing traffic numbers introduces legal liabilities if the underlying analytics tool collects personal data improperly.

### The Danger of Tracking Before Consent
Regulators actively audit websites to find third-party cookies that fire immediately on page load. Following strict enforcement of the ePrivacy Directive, authorities deploy automated crawlers to scan domains. If a crawler detects an analytics script writing a tracking ID to the browser storage without a prior click on a consent banner, the site fails the audit. Because annual GDPR violation fines routinely exceed €1 billion across the industry, sharing a dashboard powered by intrusive trackers broadcasts your non-compliance to anyone who clicks the link.

### Why Cookieless Analytics Prevents Fines
Traditional platforms like Google Analytics 4 rely on tracking cookies that require user consent. As users increasingly reject these cookies or use browser-level ad blockers, standard platforms suffer significant signal loss, which means you end up sharing a dashboard that [omits over a third of your actual visitors](https://backlinko.com/ad-blockers-users), though this rejection rate varies by industry and audience type.

Cookieless tracking resolves both the data gap and the legal risk. Swetrix processes visits using temporary, hashed identifiers, logging pageviews and aggregating data without writing files to the user's device or storing recognizable IP addresses. Bypassing the standard consent banner ensures every visitor gets counted while your shared links display anonymized behavior patterns.

## Key Features of Shareable Dashboards

Not all data belongs in public view, which means setting up a shared dashboard requires tight control over the specific metrics loaded on the screen.

### Real-Time Aggregate Metrics
Public and client-facing reports require a focus on top-level trends like total sessions, unique visitors, top referring domains, and conversion rates. Skipping granular user-level event trails prevents the exposure of confidential transaction details. Sticking to aggregated numbers aligns with GDPR data minimization rules because you only collect and share the specific metrics required to demonstrate performance.

### Tailoring Data to Your Audience
An effective dashboard highlights a maximum of 15 key performance indicators to prevent fifty different charts from overwhelming the viewer. Breaking your shared dashboard into three logical sections of acquisition, engagement, and conversion structures the information clearly. 

Display total sessions and top referrers for acquisition, track average time on page and bounce rate for engagement, and highlight macro-goals like form submissions for conversion. Executive clients prefer this high-level traffic growth, but internal teams require specific views detailing exact campaign performance. Configuring distinct dashboard views for different stakeholders keeps the data relevant for each group.

### Filtering Bot Traffic for Accurate Public Numbers
Public dashboards lose credibility when sudden spikes of bot traffic inflate the numbers. Standard platforms struggle to differentiate between genuine readers and automated scrapers, so Swetrix automatically filters known bot IPs and server-farm traffic at the network level. Verifying the legitimacy of the request before logging the pageview ensures the shared dashboard reflects human interest. This filtering prevents you from having to explain a 10,000-visit spike from a random data center to a confused client.

## How to Set Up Your Shared Analytics Dashboard

Launching a public or client-facing dashboard demands a methodical approach to data visibility.

### 1. Define Your Audience and Scope
Determine the audience for the shared link before selecting metrics. Open startups demonstrating steady growth to potential customers should include total sessions, active users, and top-performing blog posts. Excluding internal metrics like server error rates or specific geographic data prevents competitors from gaining an edge in regional advertising.

Dashboards built for clients require a focus on controlled traffic. Filtering the default view shows visits tagged with specific UTM parameters from active campaigns.

### 2. Connect Your Tracking Pixel
Collecting data safely precedes sharing it. Adding the Swetrix tracking script to the `<head>` section of your website installs a payload of a few kilobytes that loads asynchronously to protect website performance. Deploying the tag populates the dashboard with live traffic data.

### 3. Configure Privacy Filters
Navigating to project settings lets you exclude internal traffic by adding office IP addresses to the blocklist. This step prevents your team's visits from inflating the public numbers. Setting up a custom domain for tracking bypasses browser privacy blockers targeting generic third-party analytics domains.

### 4. Track Custom Events for Public Milestones
Basic pageviews provide incomplete metrics, so configuring custom events tracks specific interactions like newsletter signups, video plays, or file downloads. Swetrix logs these events without complex tag manager setups. A shared dashboard displaying a climbing graph of direct newsletter subscriptions provides concrete proof of audience engagement.

### 5. Generate the Secure Link
Opening your Swetrix project dashboard and navigating to the sharing options in project settings allows you to enable the public link feature, prompting the system to output a unique URL.

Assigning a strong password to the link protects data belonging to specific clients. Embracing transparency involves copying the open link and embedding it into the footer of your website or linking it from the company's "About" page.

### 6. Automate the Delivery
Replacing manual spreadsheet updates with an automated monthly email template delivers the password-protected link alongside a brief text summary of the top three trends. Clients learn to check the live dashboard for numbers, returning hours of administrative work to your schedule.
