Revenue attribution is the process of connecting revenue back to the channels, campaigns, pages, and touchpoints that influenced it. It goes beyond counting conversions by asking which activities produced money.

For example, a campaign may generate fewer signups than another campaign, but if those signups become higher-value customers, revenue attribution may show it is more valuable.

## Revenue attribution vs conversion attribution

Conversion attribution assigns credit for actions such as signups, leads, or purchases. Revenue attribution assigns credit for monetary value.

Both are useful, but revenue attribution is closer to business impact.

## What revenue attribution can show

Revenue attribution can show:

- Revenue by channel
- Revenue by campaign
- Revenue by landing page
- Revenue by referrer
- Revenue by content piece
- Revenue by customer segment
- Payback by campaign
- Customer value by source

This helps teams avoid optimizing only for cheap leads or high traffic volume.

## Challenges of revenue attribution

Revenue attribution can be hard because customer journeys cross devices, offline conversations, dark social, sales calls, and long time windows. No model is perfect, so attribution should guide decisions rather than pretend to be exact.
