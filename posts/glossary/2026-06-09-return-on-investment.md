Return on investment, usually shortened to ROI, is a metric that compares the value generated by an investment against its cost. In marketing analytics, ROI helps you understand whether a campaign, channel, content effort, or paid ad program is profitable.

ROI is useful because traffic alone does not prove value. A campaign can bring many visitors and still lose money if those visitors do not convert or generate enough revenue.

## ROI formula

The basic formula is:

**ROI = (return - cost) / cost x 100**

If a campaign costs $2,000 and produces $6,000 in profit or attributable value, the ROI is 200%.

Some teams calculate ROI using revenue, while others use gross profit, contribution margin, pipeline value, or customer lifetime value. The best input depends on the business model.

## ROI example

| Campaign               | Cost   | Revenue | ROI  |
| ---------------------- | ------ | ------- | ---- |
| Paid search            | $1,000 | $3,000  | 200% |
| Newsletter sponsorship | $2,000 | $2,800  | 40%  |
| Content update         | $500   | $2,500  | 400% |

ROI should be compared with payback period and confidence. Some channels produce immediate revenue. Others influence longer journeys.

## How analytics improves ROI measurement

Analytics improves ROI measurement by connecting cost, traffic, source, campaign, conversion, and revenue. UTM codes identify the campaign. Goals and events identify outcomes. Revenue analytics shows monetary value.
