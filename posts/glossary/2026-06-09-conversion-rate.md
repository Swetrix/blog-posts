Conversion rate is the percentage of visitors, sessions, or users who complete a desired action. It is one of the most important metrics in website analytics because it connects traffic to outcomes.

A conversion can be a purchase, signup, form submission, demo request, download, trial start, checkout completion, or product event. Conversion rate tells you how efficiently a page, campaign, or flow turns visitors into that outcome.

## Conversion rate formula

The basic formula is:

**Conversion rate = conversions / visitors x 100**

If 5,000 visitors land on a page and 250 sign up, the conversion rate is 5%.

Some teams calculate conversion rate by sessions instead of visitors. Others calculate it by users, leads, or accounts. The important thing is to define the denominator clearly and use it consistently.

## Conversion rate examples

| Scenario                                   | Conversion rate |
| ------------------------------------------ | --------------- |
| 100 purchases from 2,000 visitors          | 5%              |
| 50 demo bookings from 1,000 sessions       | 5%              |
| 800 email signups from 20,000 blog readers | 4%              |
| 120 upgrades from 3,000 active users       | 4%              |

Conversion rate should always be interpreted with context. A high-intent pricing page should usually convert better than an educational blog post. Branded search traffic may convert better than cold social traffic.

## How to improve conversion rate

Start by segmenting conversion rate by traffic channel, campaign, landing page, device, country, and browser. Look for patterns. A low mobile conversion rate may point to layout or speed issues. A low paid campaign conversion rate may mean the ad promise does not match the page.

Common improvements include clearer copy, better CTAs, faster page speed, reduced form friction, stronger trust signals, better pricing clarity, and A/B testing.
