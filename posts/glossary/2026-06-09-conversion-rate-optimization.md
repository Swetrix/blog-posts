Conversion rate optimization, usually shortened to CRO, is the process of improving a website, landing page, product flow, or campaign so more visitors complete a desired action. The action might be a signup, purchase, demo request, download, upgrade, or feature activation.

CRO is not just changing button colors. Good CRO combines analytics, user intent, copywriting, design, performance, experimentation, and technical quality.

## How CRO works

A practical CRO process usually follows five steps:

1. Identify pages or flows with meaningful traffic and business value.
2. Analyze where visitors drop off or fail to act.
3. Form a hypothesis about the cause.
4. Make a focused change or run an A/B test.
5. Measure the effect on conversions and guardrail metrics.

For example, if many visitors reach the pricing page but few start a trial, your hypothesis might be that plan differences are unclear. A test could simplify the pricing table, add a better comparison, or change the call to action.

## CRO metrics to track

Useful CRO metrics include:

- Conversion rate
- Click-through rate
- Bounce rate
- Funnel step completion
- Form completion rate
- Revenue per visitor
- Page speed
- JavaScript error rate

Performance and errors matter because a conversion problem is not always a messaging problem. A slow page, broken checkout, or JavaScript error can hurt conversion rate even when the offer is strong.

## What CRO is not

CRO is not about tricking visitors into clicking. Short-term tricks can increase clicks while damaging trust, retention, or revenue quality. Better CRO helps visitors understand the offer, reduce uncertainty, and complete the action they already came to consider.
