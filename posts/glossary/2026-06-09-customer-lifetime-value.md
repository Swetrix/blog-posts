Customer lifetime value, often shortened to CLV or LTV, is the estimated total revenue or gross profit a customer generates over their relationship with a business. It helps teams understand how much a customer is worth beyond the first purchase or signup.

CLV is especially important for subscription businesses, SaaS products, ecommerce stores, and any company with repeat purchases.

## Simple CLV formula

A simple formula is:

**CLV = average revenue per customer x average customer lifetime**

For a subscription product, teams may use monthly recurring revenue, gross margin, churn rate, and expansion revenue to estimate CLV more accurately.

## Why CLV matters

CLV helps answer:

- How much can we spend to acquire a customer?
- Which channels bring high-value customers?
- Which segments retain better?
- Which campaigns create profitable growth?
- Which onboarding steps influence long-term value?

Without CLV, teams may optimize only for cheap signups instead of valuable customers.
