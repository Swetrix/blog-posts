A page title is the HTML title that describes a web page. It appears in browser tabs and can appear as the clickable headline in search results. In SEO, it is often called the title tag.

Page titles help search engines and users understand what a page is about.

## Page title example

A page title can look like this:

```html
<title>Privacy-first Web Analytics | Swetrix</title>
```

Good page titles are specific, readable, and aligned with search intent.

## Why page titles matter

Page titles can affect both search relevance and click-through rate. A clear title helps searchers decide whether the page answers their query.

Poor titles can make pages harder to understand, reduce CTR, and create duplicate or confusing search snippets.

## How to analyze page title performance

Use search analytics to look for pages with high impressions and low CTR. Compare title wording, query intent, average position, and landing page conversion.
